The project

Project Description

Quizzaris is a study app that allows students to collaborate and test each other with questions created by themselves, tutors and lecturers. They can choose between categories and take quizzes in a flashcard style to revise material or submit their own questions based on subject and topic. The app will allow students to take quizzes in topics of their interest. Upon checking an answer, statistics like the percentage of users who chose which answers, its explanations and solution discussions will be shown. A user will have a profile area so that progression is recorded and made available for each person that logs in. Quizzes, answers, and comments can be voted by anyone; some people may have special attributes that enables moderator clearance. Based on personal progress, students will be able to access information on themselves such as which areas need improvement. And on top of that, study plans can be created and synced with university calendars and timetables. Ultimately, its main goal is to make student lives easier, and create a friendly, efficient learning environment. Some user stories can be used to further the understanding of the project. They will be developed in the following lines.

  • As an international student that recently came to Melbourne, I am having trouble keeping my study schedule up to date - especially considering all events unfolding because of COVID. I am not used to having online classes, since I have always relied heavily on face to face conversations and study sessions with my friends and tutors. Having an intuitive layout with quick quizzes and online discussion about it would help keep me on track.
  • As a tutor, I am used to seeing student reactions and can usually have a good guess about how well the class content is being absorbed. However, I feel a lack of home-based study for most of my pupils. Being able to monitor overall student progress in a unified study platform, especially on very specific topics, would allow me to better prepare class materials and assignment specifications.
  • As a history student, I like to explore different subjects. Recently I have started some design courses and was eventually led to web programming. Through research, I feel that most of the free content available is not very well structured for someone from a completely unrelated field. Moreover, I do not adapt very well to the structure of posting questions and waiting for a reply on web forums. I would be very happy if I had some means of doing a quick study in targeted areas with the possibility of discussions with other pupils.
  • As a lecturer, I want to make sure that students are covering the correct content and doing questions related to the course when they do quizzes so I want to be able to create and moderate the kind of questions, solutions and comments posted on quizzes to make sure they are not doing things off topic, not posting offensive content and to make sure that people are not mislead by incorrect solution explanations.

features

features

1. Users can do quizzes and see their score

It can be stated that this is the main feature of the project. Users will be able to choose quizzes based on subject topics, see solutions with explanations after answering them and have a record of their scores and attempts. Within the quiz, number of questions and difficulty level will be displayed.

Feature validation

  • Doing the quiz but leaving halfway through will prompt an alert that they are leaving the quiz. It will resume in the same point when returned to.
  • Attempting to take the same quiz again will alert that the user has attempted it before but allow them to proceed.
  • Unregistered users may take quizzes and view solutions, but their scores will not be saved. A warning message on attempt/completion will appear stating that results are only saved for registered users.

2. Users should register to be able submit questions and solutions

Registered users will be able to write their own questions. Submissions may be drafted should the user choose not to submit it immediately and rated by a Turn it in style system to alert about copies or similarity levels. Questions will have ratings, difficulty levels and suggestions for similar ones based on subject. Immediately after submission, the author will be directed to its post and have access to answers history.

Feature validation

  • Submissions of questions and answers are entered in the database.
  • Blank submissions are not added and get and error alert.
  • Non-registered users will receive a “Login required” on attempting to submit questions and answers.
  • Incoherent or irrelevant submissions are flagged for further review before being accepted.

3. Users can see achievements, scores, and their progression

Registered users will have access to personal data highlighting progression, grades, scores, and attempt history in a written and graphical manner. Upon quiz completion, general and individual quiz scores are displayed.

Feature validation

  • Unregistered users will not have access to scores. On attempting to view scores a prompt will be shown asking for login.
  • Registered users will have access to their all previous scores. If there are none, nothing will be displayed.

4. Registered users can leave comments and discuss about the quiz under solutions

Upon quiz completion, registered users will be able to scroll, edit, delete, and vote the comments that appear below the answers. Comments will have parent and child chains and a flag icon that can be used to alerts moderators.

Feature validation

  • Users will have access to comments and discussions after answering the quiz. Prompt for access denied when trying to view results before completing the quiz
  • Unregistered users can view answers but not write comments.
  • If a registered user tries to submit a blank comment, a message will be displayed stating that no submission will be made. If the comment contains blacklisted words such as inappropriate ones, the comment will not be submitted, and a prompt will be displayed stating that the comment contains blacklisted words. If a comment appears to be nonsensical or is voted as off topic, it will be hidden and flagged for further inspection from moderators.
  • If a user repeatedly flags comments that turns out to be acceptable, a time-based restriction will be put into place denying them access to comment or flag. If an attempt to flag is made during that period, a prompt will be shown indicating the time left before they may do it again. Likewise, if multiple flags are put against the same user, the same restriction will apply.
  • If a user re offends, the cool down period will increase, and after the third time a prompt will appear indication that the next offence will lead to permanent banishment.

5. Registered users can rate questions in terms of difficulty and whether the question was well written or not.

A question rating system will work based on popular voting. Should enough users flag as “needs improvement”, the question will be hidden, and a message will be sent to the author to revise it. Moderators may also do modifications. The most up voted ones will come to the top sections, and the down votes will push it down. Besides up and down votes, difficulty ranking can also be established as beginner, intermediate, advanced.

Feature validation

  • Unregistered used will be denied the ability to rate question difficulty and quality
  • Registered users will only be allowed to change their votes once
  • Edited questions will have their ratings reset
  • Up voting enough may mark the question for review by moderation

Extended Features

1. Registered users can create study plans based on university calendar or timetable.

User area can be synced with university timetables and schedules through Google and Outlook calendars which be updated through .cal files. Study schedules can be mutable according to student progression and topic relevance. Study session efficiency is measured according to quiz scores, and areas in need of improvement highlighted based on topics of wrongly answered questions.

  • If a user is not registered with a student email, they will not be able to sync timetables and will only be able to manually input timetable info and assignment times/topics.
  • Unregistered users will not have access to this function.
  • If a user is registered with a student email but fails to sync timetable data, they will be given the option to try again or input manually.

2. Registered users can compare their scores and performance against an aggregate of other users.

User profiles will contain personal scores that can be set to visible or hidden. Higher level users bypass hidden scores, and friends can compare score progression based on subject topics.

  • If an unregistered user attempts to view scores of other users they will be denied, shown an error message indicating that they cannot view it due to not being a registered user. If they attempt to message or contact said user they will be denied and the message not sent.
  • If a registered user attempts to view other users scores, they will be shown an overview of the user's profile. If the user attempts to look at individual scores and private information they will be denied access and show a message that they do not have high enough privilege to view this information. If the other user has privacy settings on, attempting to view their scores will prompt a message that their profile is private, and you cannot view their info. If the other user has privacy settings on, they can still be messaged and sending a message to another unknown registered user will be processed.
  • A registered user that is certified a high level user (moderator, lecturer) can view all information.

3. Registered users can compare their scores and performance against an aggregate of other users.

Different permissions allow users to perform different actions, like editing, deleting, or flagging comments, creating questions and quizzes, prioritizing comments and votes, making announcements, changes to subject categories, or even banning other users.

  • A registered user that is certified a high-level user (moderator, lecturer) can view all information of others.
  • Base level users can access quizzes, create questions and answers. They will not have to option visible to them to edit other users' comments.
  • If a user registers and applies for higher level clearance but does not supply ID, they are denied. If they supply their university email and university ID and a universally recognised ID, it will be processed and checked by someone in admin before their clearance is given.
  • If a user attempts to impersonate a higher-level person for clearance, both accounts will be suspended until admin can confirm ID of the account owner.

Project motivation

Describe what motivated your group to choose this project?

The group started off with the idea of an app related to study or productivity because of the very well-known struggles associated with studying and university life. During a brainstorm session, the popular website Kahoot! was briefly mentioned. It is basically an online quiz game: an admin provides the users with a code that can be used to join a room. Inside it, a question followed by four flashcards appear on screen under a timer and whoever gets the rights answers faster wins. However, the application was greatly gamified and resulted in students who are not serious to ruin the learning experienced for the other students. Not only did this hinder the learning process for the students but also distracted the teachers from focusing on class. Another application that the team had a look at was piazza which allows students to sign up and participate in quizzes with the team. However, the application does not let a student sign up without a valid classroom. This results in the students being unable to sign up and practice without being a part of a class. That proved to be the inspiration that kick started the project and set us off with the idea of educational quizzes facilitated by the use of flashcards, which answers could be uploaded by students or lecturers, progress measured, content voted and synced with universities content and course structures.
